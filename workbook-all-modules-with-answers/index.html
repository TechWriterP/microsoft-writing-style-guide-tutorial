Module 3: Grammar and Parts of Speech

üåü Learning objectives:

  • Use present tense, active voice, and second person consistently.
  • Recognize and correct subject-verb agreement issues.
  • Apply the indicative and imperative moods appropriately.

‚úçÔ∏è Section 1: Present Tense

Explanation: Use the present tense to describe features, procedures, and factual information. It makes content feel immediate and relevant.

Practice ‚Äì Convert to present tense: 1. The system was scanning files.

  1. You will receive an email notification.

  2. The guide was written to help users.

  3. After you logged in, the dashboard was displayed.

  4. The process had completed.

üîÑ Section 2: Active Voice

Explanation: In active voice, the subject performs the action. It‚Äôs clearer and more direct than passive voice.

Practice ‚Äì Convert to active voice: 1. The form was submitted by the user.

  1. The account has been verified.

  2. The file was created automatically.

  3. The password was changed.

  4. The error message is displayed when the page loads.

üîÄ Section 3: Indicative and Imperative Mood

Explanation: Use indicative mood for stating facts. Use imperative mood when giving instructions.

Practice: 1. Convert to imperative: "You should enter a name in the field."

  1. Convert to indicative: "Click the button."

  2. Convert to imperative: "You can set your preferences in Settings."

  3. Convert to indicative: "Restart the app."

  4. Convert to imperative: "It‚Äôs a good idea to reboot the app."

üßÆ Section 4: Use Second Person (you)

Explanation: Speak directly to the reader using "you." It creates a more personal and helpful tone.

Practice ‚Äì Convert to second person: 1. Users must complete the form.

  1. The administrator sets permissions for the user.

  2. Customers can view their order history.

  3. Participants should log in on time.

  4. The user should reset the password.

üî¢ Section 5: Subject-Verb Agreement

Explanation: Match verbs to their subjects in number and person.

Practice ‚Äì Fix the subject-verb agreement: 1. Your files is saved.

  3. Your browser and your device supports syncing.

  4. One of the features are missing.
